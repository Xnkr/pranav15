    LINE FOLLOWER is one of the very simplest robotic event. Here a robot built by the participating team must sense the black line marked on the track and must reach the destination surpassing the hurdles and obstacles on the way. The robot must be preprogramed and must run independently.

    GENERAL RULES:

  • The participants should bring their college ID cards for identification purpose.
  • Participants must report on or before 9.00 am.

  • Only one team member will be allowed in the track.

  • Lunch will not be provided.

    • EVENT RULES:

  • The time measured by the coordinators will be final and will be used to judge the teams.
  • The event coordinators receive the right to make modifications to any of the rules if deemed necessary and their decision in any situation is final.
  • The starting procedure of the robot should be simple and can involve giving the bot two manual interventions, beyond which leads to disqualification.
  • There will be preliminary & final rounds, depending upon the number of teams, qualification will be done accordingly.
  • All teams will be given a calibration time of 15 minutes before the round starts to get their bot ready.
  • The points would be given, i.e., for
  • Tough curves =+20 pts
  • Interventions=-20 pts
  • The teams getting through the track in the least amount of time and least number of touches will be announced as the winner.

    • BOT SPECIFICATION:

  • A bot must fit within a square dimension of 30cm x 30cm. There are no height constraints.
  • The bot must use an on-board power supply. No external power supply will be allowed.
  • The bot must be completely autonomous and it should not receive any external help during the event.
  • The potential difference between any two points in the robot should not exceed 12V DC.

  • The designed track might consist of straight lines, smooth curves, obtuse turns, right angle turns, acute turns, breaks and the track is a black line on a white background. The width of the line is about 3 Cm. The distance between any two adjacent tracks will be 30 cm.

    • RC RACING

    “When in doubt, throttle it out.”

    This event is a break for the car enthusiasts and the avid gamers alike. A course is built specifically for the race between remote controlled cars powered by battery and built by the participants themselves.

    INSPECTION:

    • 1. Participants have to report to the venue with their machines.
    • 2. Inspection of the machine will be carried out according to the guidelines & regulations set by the organizers. Points will be reduced in case the rules are violated.
    • 3. All selected teams will be given practice slots of 1 lap each, after the inspection is completed.

    TRACK SPECIFICATIONS:

    • 1. The race track will be an off-road arena.
    • 2. The race track will be a dirt track with turns, bends, pits, bumps, puddles, hairpin bends and vertical U turn.
    • 3. An elevated podium will be provided for controlling the machine. The podium will ensure good visibility for the controller

    MACHINE SPECIFICATIONS:

    • 1. The machine must be necessarily controlled by a wireless remote control mechanism throughout the race.
    • 2. Any team found using a machine with a ready-made chassis will be disqualified.

    RACE RULES:

  • A lap is considered to be completed when the machine completes the entire track & comes back to the starting point.
  • The track will have check points at regular intervals. If a machine halts, or goes off the arena at any point on the track, one of the participants is allowed to place it at the nearest checkpoint. The delay time will not be deducted from the lap time.
  • If any of the machines starts off before the flag is waved, the timer would be restarted and the machines will get a second chance. However, if any machine starts off before the waving of flag for a second time, it will be disqualified. No re-match will be held for the second time.
  • Team members are not permitted to touch either their machines once the race begins (unless there is need to place the machine at the check point). If found not obeying the rules the team will be disqualified.
  • Teams are not allowed to intentionally damage the machine of the opponent's team. If found doing so on track (while racing), the concerned team will be disqualified. Execution of this rule will be subjective and relies completely on judge's discretion.
  • Once the race starts, only one of the team members has the right to re-position the car, none of the team members should enter the track except that particular person.
  • Judge's decision will be treated as final no discrepancy shall be entertained.

    • GENERAL RULES:

  • Pre-Registration is Mandatory. No spot registrations are allowed.
  • The participants should bring their college ID cards for identification purpose.
  • Participants must report on or before 9.00 am.
  • Each team can consist of only a maximum of two participants.
  • The robot must be built by the participants themselves, if not the team stands disqualified.
  • The participants must not exceed the given time limit.
  • The participants must adhere to the rules and regulations formed by the symposium committee members and volunteers.
  • Lunch will not be provided.
  • Students with a valid identity card from their respective institutions are only eligible for participation.
  • A team is allowed to drive only one car.
  • If the machine is found unsafe to run on the track or found to be damaging the track that team will be disqualified from the race. The organizers’ decision will be final and binding in this respect.
  • The teams must adhere to the spirit of healthy competition. The organizers reserve the right to disqualify any team indulged in misbehavior.

    Clue it is a technical event that tests your skills and ability to understand words and phrases said indirectly. Participants would be judged based on their understanding capacity, speed and communication capabilities.

  • The event is a team event with four rounds.
  • ROUND 1: Each team will be given 10 words and the time limit is 90 seconds. Based on the scores and time taken, teams will be selected for the next round.
  • ROUND 2: Each team will be given 10 words and the time limit is 60 seconds. Based on the scores and time taken, teams will be selected for the next round.
  • ROUND 3: Within the duration of 60 seconds, participants must memorize bunch of words and clues must be given to the team mate. There is no time split for the words. Based on the scores and time, taken teams will be selected for the next round.
  • ROUND 4: Each team will be shown a picture and a word related to the original word. Within the duration of 45 seconds, the team must decode maximum number of questions (out of 10).
  • TIE BREAKER: It will be a buzzer round and one question will be asked by the coordinator and the team which presses the buzzer first will be given the first preference and if answered correctly will be announced as the winner, if not the question will be passed to the team which pressed successively.
